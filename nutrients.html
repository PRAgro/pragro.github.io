  • Nutrient Requirement in Plants

    Nutrients play a crucial role in plant growth and development in farming. They provide essential elements that plants need to carry out various physiological processes, such as photosynthesis, nutrient absorption, and overall metabolism. By supplying the right balance of nutrients, farmers can ensure optimal plant health, increased yields, and improved crop quality.

  • Primary Nutrients

    Primary nutrients, also known as macronutrients, are essential elements required by plants in large quantities for their growth and development. These primary nutrients include Nitrogen, Phosphorus, and Potassium (NPK). Nitrogen is crucial for promoting leafy green growth and overall plant vigor. Phosphorus plays a key role in root development, flowering, and fruiting. Potassium helps in enhancing disease resistance, improving drought tolerance, and promoting overall plant health. Balancing the levels of these primary nutrients is vital for ensuring optimal crop yield and quality in modern agriculture.

  • Secondary Nutrients

    Secondary nutrients, such as calcium, magnesium, and sulfur, play a crucial role in plant growth. Calcium is essential for cell wall structure and stability, as well as for nutrient uptake and enzyme activity. Magnesium is a component of chlorophyll, which is vital for photosynthesis and overall plant growth. Sulfur is important for amino acid production, protein synthesis, and overall plant health. Ensuring adequate levels of these secondary nutrients in the soil is essential for maximizing crop quality and productivity.

  • Micro-Nutrients

    Micro nutrients play a crucial role in fertigation as they are essential for the overall health and growth of plants. Ferrous helps in the formation of chlorophyll and improves the plant's ability to utilize nutrients efficiently. Copper is important for enzyme activation and plays a role in photosynthesis. Zinc is necessary for hormone regulation and protein synthesis. Manganese aids in photosynthesis and nitrogen metabolism. Molybdenum is essential for nitrogen fixation and helps in enzyme activation. Chloride regulates stomatal opening and closing, while boron is crucial for cell wall formation and sugar transport within the plant.
